Tax cuts to scale

Let's talk about scale.

Last year, the BoM announced an unfortunate rebrand that was universally condemned as wasteful spending.

But the total cost to the taxpayer came in at a meagre $220 thousand.

Compare that to the $5 billion we spent not buying nuclear submarines from France, and, well, you can’t really.

You could rebrand the BoM once a day for 68 years for that much money, and yet the two stories got similar amounts of coverage.

Human brains aren't well suited to dealing with large sums of cash, which becomes a problem when we want to have a conversation about how and where we spend Australia’s money.

So, with the stage 3 tax cuts due to kick in soon, we thought we’d give you a hand putting their price tag into perspective.

The budget office estimates that they will open up a $243 billion hole in the national budget over the next ten years.
