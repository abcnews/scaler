The princess and the price tag

When Jim Chalmers made his budget speech to parliament in October, he promised the spending was going to be “responsible, not reckless.”

Then he threw out a bunch of figures, most of which were in the billions.

We’re used to working with budgets in the hundreds and thousands of dollars – for the lucky among us, we'll buy a house for more than a million from time to time.

But when prices get much bigger than that, we lose all sense of scale.

How are we to judge whether spending $7.5 billion on cost of living relief is "responsible" or "reckless"?

One of the most controvserial items on the government's balance sheet – the coming stage 3 tax cuts – are estimated to cost $243 billion over the next ten years.

That's more than double the amount earmarked for transport infrastructure over the same time period.

It would take the average Australian over two million years to earn that much at their day job. Or, if we were in a James Bond movie, it’d fill about one thousand briefcases stacked with $100 bills.

Those comparisons really aren't helping, so let's have a real go at putting $243 billion into perspective.
